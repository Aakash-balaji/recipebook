Veg Briyani

Additional information
  • Prep TIme: 15min
  • Cooking Time: 45min
  • Total Time: 1 houre
  • Servings:6

To make veg briyani follow the INSTRUCTIONS:

INGEDIENTS:

  • 1 cup basmati rice, soaked for 20 to 30 minutes
  • 1/4 cup onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green chilli, slit or chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp mint leaves, chopped
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 cups water
  • Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamoms, 1 inch cinnamon, 1/4 tsp shahi jeera
  • Vegetables: 1/4 cup cauliflower florets, 1/4 cup potato cubes, 1/4 cup carrot cubes, 1/4 cup green peas, 8 to 10 cashews
  • Biryani masala: 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp red chilli powder, 1/2 tsp garam masala, 1/4 tsp coriander powder, a pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp hot water
  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker and fry the cashews until golden. Remove and keep aside.
  2. In the same oil or ghee, add the whole spices and fry for a few seconds until aromatic.
  3. Add the onion, green chilli and ginger garlic paste and saute until the onion turns golden brown.
  4. Add the vegetables and salt and mix well. Cook for about 5 minutes on a medium flame.
  5. Add the biryani masala and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Add the rice and water and bring to a boil. Lower the flame and cover the cooker with a lid. Pressure cook for one whistle on a low flame.
  7. Turn off the flame and let the pressure release naturally.
  8. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with mint leaves, coriander leaves and fried cashews. Serve hot with raita or salan.

Veg manchurian

Additional information
  • Prep Time: 15min
  • Cook Time: 30min
  • Total Time: 55min
  • Yield: 2-3 Servings

To make veg manchurian follow the following steps

INGEDIENTS:

  • 1/4 cup grated cabbage
  • 1/4 cup grated carrot
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped capsicum
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped spring onion
  • 1 tsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1 tsp finely chopped garlic
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tbsp maida
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp vinegar
  • 2 tsp chilli sauce
  • 2 tsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tsp cornflour dissolved in 2 tbsp water

    steps to make manchurian

  1. In a large bowl, mix cabbage, carrot, capsicum, spring onion, ginger, garlic, cornflour, maida, salt and pepper. Make small balls from the mixture and deep fry them in hot oil until golden and crisp. Drain on a paper towel and keep aside.
  2. In another pan, heat some oil and saute garlic, ginger and spring onion for a few minutes. Add soy sauce, vinegar, chilli sauce, tomato ketchup, sugar, salt and pepper and mix well. Add water and bring to a boil.
  3. Add the cornflour slurry and stir continuously until the sauce thickens. Adjust the seasoning as per your taste.
  4. Add the fried veg balls to the sauce and toss gently to coat them well. Garnish with some spring onion greens and serve hot with fried rice or noodles.

Mysore Masala Dosa

Additional information
  • Prep Time: 40 Mins
  • Cook Time: 30 Mins
  • Resting Time: 720 Mins
  • Servings: 12

INGEDIENTS:

For Batter

  • 1/2 cup Black Gram
  • 1 cup Idly Rice
  • 1 cup Dosa Rice
  • 1 tsp Fenugreek Seeds
  • 2 tsp Raw Bengal Gram
  • 1/2 cup Thick Rice Flakes
  • Water To grind the Batter
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil to roast the Dosa
  • Butter/Ghee adequate

For masala

  • 15 Byadgi chilli (soaked in hot Water)
  • 2 tsp Raw Bengal Gram
  • 1/4 cup Chopped Onions
  • 1 tsp Sesame Seeds
  • 5 - 6 Garlic Cloves
  • 1/8 tsp Turmeric
  • Salt
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 2 tbsp Oil

For potato masala

  • 3 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Raw Bengal Gram
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 Spring Curry Leaves
  • 1 cup Chopped Onions
  • 1.5 tsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 1/4 tsp Turmeric
  • Salt 4 Boiled Potatoes
  • 1/4 cup Water
  • 1/4 cup Chopped Green Coriander
  • 2 tbsp Green Chillies chopped

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Wash all the ingredients for the Dosa Batter, soak them for at least 5 hours and then grind to a smooth paste in a mixie. (See tips)
  2. Let the Batter ferment for at least 12 hours.
  3. After 12 hours, take a sufficient Batter, add salt to taste and dilute it with water to the desired consistency
  4. Heat Oil for the Masala, add Bengal Gram and roast till golden. Then add the chopped Onions, Sesame Seeds and cook until the onions turn soft. Then add Garlic finally and remove it from the fire.
  5. Add the chopped Onions and fry for 2-3 minutes. Then add the Ginger Garlic Paste, Turmeric and Salt and fry for another 2 minutes.
  6. Mash the boiled potatoes and add it to the mixture. Sprinkle a little water and fry until the Water evaporates. Add Green Coriander and Green Chillies finally and remove them from the fire.
  7. Before you spread the Batter, put some oil on the pan, rub it well with an Onion and wipe off the excess oil.
  8. Then add the Dosa Batter and spread it thin.
  9. As soon as you pour the Batter, put some Masala paste in the center and spread it across the Dosa. Pour Oil along the edges of the Dosa and add a blob of butter in the center. Let the Dosa roast to a crispy finish without touching it with a flat ladle.
  10. Do not remove the Dosa until it is well roasted. Once it is roasted crisp, set the Potato Curry in the middle, fold the Dosa and then remove it from the pan and serve.

Now your Dosa is ready to eat
